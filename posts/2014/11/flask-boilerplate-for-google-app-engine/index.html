Flask boilerplate for google app engine

Sun 30 November 2014

It has been over a year that I started writing codes and midst of that journey I felt the need to deploy some of codes on a server. And there I started experimenting with different cloud hosting platform to deploy my apps. Starting with Heroku, I tried Amazon AWS and finally Google App Engine.

I will not go deep into explaining the differences between those three cloud providers. Just that for a beginner AWS proves to be a little too much to configure. It took me days to understand and eventually deploy the app. But then Heroku and GAE are much better options to test the waters.

Just like other Google services GAE is also very easy to use, although there are certain limitations interms of language and the database but then every cloud service has its baggage.

Having spent consedrable amount of time with Django, it was a natural choice to go forward. BUt if you have ever used GAE you would know that Django is probably not a right choice for GAE. It has been said that the strengths of these technologies do not align well. You tend to loose out on some of the wonderful features of Django on GAE.

A little more digging around gave me a simpler Flask boilerplate for GAE. Ofcource it has been modified to leverage the model-view-controller logic from Django.

I have hosted few static websites using the same and it was done in almost notime. I have still not used the models to interact with the database and will be my next target
