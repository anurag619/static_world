Starting out with Django (my experience)

Sat 17 January 2015

This post is not about how/why one should work on Django. Rather its a small recollection of my memories when I started with this popular Python framwork.

Today, I was looking to start a new freelance project and thought Django will probably fit in the requirements very well. The very first thing I needed was the latest Django code and well, djangoproject is the right place to look for the same. A stream of old memories struck me at the very instant.

Starting out with Django was not so good experince for me, I had really tough time learning the basics. It was not before 3 attempts (and 1 year) that I realized how things work in Django. I would spend long hours on the djangoproject site trying to gulp what the tutorial exactly meant.

Man, it was tough, considering the fact that I had little Python experince and I was in a hurry to create a web portal. Someone told (well, i read it on the internet) me Django is a good web framework. Looking back it now, I feel I should have started out with Flask first. But instead i learned flask after experimenting with Django. As there was no one to guide me about the basics I made my own sets of mistakes. But hey! I learned a lot as well so I would reer them as my leaning curve :D .

Present

Django 1.7 is now the latest version and I am sure things must have improved a lot. I know I am probably very late to even read the new features.

I last experimented with Django 1.5, when I was building an initial prototype for geeklab (the project never got completed) I was totally in favour of Django over Flask. But with latest developments in Betaglide (yeah, thats where I work) and my learning with Google Appengine, I would say Flask is impressive as well. I guess every framwork has its own merits and its upto us how/where we use them.

Ok, enough of blabbering, I should get back to my project now.
